Places: Profile - Location (8)   Resume (33)   Profile - Location   Canberra: Gungahlin Homestead (ACT)The Gungahlin Homestead in the Australian Capital Territory, is an historic site in the north of Canberra that now serves as head office for CSIRO Sustainable Ecosystems. Canberra: Black Mountain laboratories (ACT)CSIRO Black Mountain laboratories in Canberra have facilities servicing insect, plant, atmospheric, land and water science. Alice Springs: Centre for Arid Zone Research (NT)CSIRO’s Centre for Arid Zone Research is conducting research into regional futures, indigenous livelihoods, conservation planning and management, sustainable agricultural systems and knowledge management for arid rangelands. Townsville: Davies Laboratory (Qld)The Davies Laboratory is located in Townsville, North Queensland, serves as base for researchers from CSIRO Sustainable Ecosystems, CSIRO Land and Water and CSIRO Plant Industry. Floreat: Centre for Environment and Life Sciences (CELS) (WA)CSIRO Centre for Environment and Life Sciences is home to 300 scientists conducting researchto ensure an environmentally sustainable Australia and promoting and maintaining good health. Narrabri: Australian Cotton Research Institute (NSW)Discover CSIRO’s hub of cotton research, which is breeding new cotton varieties, developing sustainable cropping systems and improving pest management for agribusiness. Brisbane: Queensland Bioscience Precinct (Qld)Research partnerships such as those at the Queensland Bioscience Precinct will provide solutions to the major problems facing the nation and the world. Toowoomba: Agricultural Production Systems Research Unit (Qld)Based in Toowoomba, Queensland, the Agricultural Production Systems Research Unit (APSRU) is a collaborative organisation with researchers from CSIRO, the University Of Queensland and the Queensland Departments of Primary Industries and Fisheries and Natural Resources and Mines. Resume   Dr Andrew Ash: helping manage the landDr Andrew Ash leads the Rangelands and Savannas program at CSIRO Sustainable Ecosystems addressing management of arid, semi-arid and tropical rangelands. Dr Geoff Baker: developing management strategies for invertebratesDr Baker is the Theme Leader of Biological Drivers for Agricultural Sustainability, whose research on insect pests in cotton aims to help prolong the life of Bt transgenic cottons.   Dr David Boyle: improving the diagnosis of infectious animal diseasesDr Boyle is a virologist with extensive expertise in developing diagnostic tests and vaccines for a range of animal diseases, working at the Australian Animal Health Laboratory (AAHL) in Geelong, Victoria. Dr Rob Bramley: understanding vineyard variabilityDr Rob Bramley’s work on precision viticulture is internationally recognised for reducing uncertainty and increasing productivity for grapegrowers and winemakers. Dr Greg Constable: leading cotton researchDr Greg Constable is a leader in cotton research, investigating plant breeding, higher yield management packages, improved sustainability, reducing insecticide use and genetically modified cotton varieties. Dr Paul De Barro: working with industry to combat invasive speciesAs well as research on silverleaf whitefly and the viruses it transmits, Dr Paul De Barro is heavily involved in Australian biosecurity planning and invasive species.   Dr Manny Delhaize: unravelling acid soil toleranceDr Manny Delhaize is working to improve the acid soil tolerance of crops and pastures. Dr Margaret Friedel: contributing to arid land sustainabilityDr Margaret Friedel is using modelling techniques to help outback communities in areas such as tourism sustainability and management of exotic buffel grass. Dr Peter Hunt: improving control of ruminant parasitesDr Peter Hunt is working to manage livestock parasites and is based at the FD McMaster Laboratory in Armidale, New South Wales. Dr Geoff Inman-Bamber: improving production efficiencies in the sugar industryDr Inman-Bamber’s research on sugarcane is internationally recognised.  He is working to provide a better understanding of sugarcane physiology to advance the global production of sugarcane. Dr Martyn Jeggo: controlling exotic and emerging animal diseaseDr Martyn Jeggo leads the Australian Animal Health Laboratory (AAHL), which is a global leader in research and diagnosis of major diseases affecting livestock throughout the world, including new and emerging diseases. Dr Andrew Johnson: Acting Executive Director, Strategic Change ProgramsDr Andrew Johnson is a past Chief of CSIRO Sustainable Ecosystems and is currently on full-time secondment to the CSIRO Executive Team in the role of Acting Executive Director - Strategic Change Programs. Mr Allen Kearns: environmental scientist and industrial ecologistMr Allen Kearns, Deputy Chief, Business Integration at CSIRO Sustainable Ecosystems, is responsible for a wide range of strategic science planning, business development, science consultancies, and corporate governance activities within the division. Dr Brian Keating: Chief, CSIRO Sustainable EcosystemsDr Brian Keating is an expert in farming systems science with broad experience researching sustainable land management. Responsible for strategic science development and research operations across the division, he also manages the division's external relationships. Dr Rob Kelly: improving performance of livestock and related industriesDr Rob Kelly has a wealth of research experience in improving the productivity and environmental performance of small ruminant industries in Mediterranean and temperate climates. Dr John Kirkegaard: improving farm productivity and sustainabilityDr John Kirkegaard is sub-program leader of CSIRO Plant Industry's Innovations in Profitable Mixed Farming team. Dr Shimin Liu: improving livestock health, performance and product qualityDr Shimin Liu's research focusses on improving ruminant production and producing healthier animal products for consumers.
