Cold water eddy ‘monsters” mighty current off Sydney (Podcast 20 Mar 07)In this six-minute podcast Dr David Griffin, from the Wealth from Ocean's Flagship, explains how a huge dense eddy was the reason the sea was so cold off the coast of NSW this summer. Climate change view clearer with new oceans satellite (Media release 12 Mar 07)Australian scientists will have access to the most detailed measurements of ocean circulation and global sea level variations following the launch next year of a multinational ocean-observing satellite – Jason-2. Space scientists to take the Earth’s pulse (Media release 8 Mar 07)Dozens of international satellite and modelling experts are meeting in Canberra today to discuss how to improve observations of the Earth to better understand and predict climate change, water availability, and natural disasters. DataTraceDNA takes up fight against fake art fraudsters (Podcast 2 Mar 07)Mr Greg Twemlow from DataTraceDNA explains how technology developed by CSIRO is tackling the problem of fake art in this six-minute podcast. Millimetre-wave imagingThe multi-spectral imaging project is building scanners which use millimetre-waves to 'see' through smoke, dust, fog and clothing, in daylight or darkness. CSIRO and Chinese research corporation partner to commercialise wireless technology (Media release 1 Mar 07)The CSIRO ICT Centre has signed a landmark contract to commercialise its advanced technologies in China. Dr Andre Zerger: managing resources with spatial technologyDr Andre Zerger is a spatial analyst with a broad research interest in applying geospatial technologies to natural resource management challenges and spatial decision support. CSIRO imagery shows outer Great Barrier Reef at risk from river plumes (Media release 22 Feb 07)A stunning series of satellite imagery of Australia’s Great Barrier Reef released by the CSIRO shows for the first time visual confirmation of the theory that sediment plumes travel to the outer reef, and beyond. Keeping track of Australia’s water (Podcast 20 Feb 07)Dr Rob Vertessy discusses the Water Resources Observation Network (WRON) in this five-minute podcast, and why it’s so important to Australia. Dr Michael Barber: Group Executive for CSIRO’s Information, Manufacturing &amp; Minerals group.In addition to his research in statistical mechanics, material science and computational mathematics, Dr Barber is well-known for his contributions to the development of Australian science policy. CSIRO and University of Tasmania build technology strength for Tasmania (Media release 13 Feb 07)CSIRO and the University of Tasmania today signed a significant agreement to develop the research leaders of tomorrow. Robotics student workshop (Northern Territory)Teach something else to do your work for you. CSIRO runs an interactive robotics program for students in Years 3-6 in the Northern Territory . DesignCheck™: state and national building industry award winnerDesignCheck™ is an automated design checking system enabling quick and easy compliance assessment against building codes helping designers to find potential problems early. CSIRO leads water information initiative (Media release 1 Feb 07)CSIRO researchers have taken part in an international demonstration of a key technology for monitoring Australia’s scarce water resources. Mr Kevin Cryan: supporting CSIRO’s business activities in mathematics and statisticsMr Kevin Cryan leads the Business Development and Commercialisation team at CSIRO Mathematical and Information Sciences. The team delivers technology solutions based on mathematics and statistics. Mr Ross Ackland: working for the sustainable management of our water resourcesMr Ross Ackland is helping to revolutionise Australia’s ability to monitor, forecast and manage water demand, supply, quality and use patterns with the Water Resources Observation Network (WRON). Dr Margaret Friedel: contributing to arid land sustainabilityDr Margaret Friedel is using modelling techniques to help outback communities in areas such as tourism sustainability and management of exotic buffel grass. Dr Lan Ding: improving the built environmentVirtual environment and 3D simulations, multi-agent models and facilities management are key aspects of Dr Ding’s research on improving the built environment. Molecular electronicsThe continuing trend of miniaturising electronics means we will soon reach the scale of molecules and atoms. Molecular electronics promises to be cheaper and might replace some silicon-based technologies within 15 years. CSIRO wins transfer of US WLAN court case (Media release 18 Dec 06)A federal court in California has agreed with CSIRO that upcoming US WLAN patent cases brought against CSIRO should be heard in Texas by the same court that recently found in favour of CSIRO against Buffalo companies. Gigabit wireless breakthrough (Podcast 13 Dec 06)Dr Jay Guo discusses a new wireless six gigabit connection which uses millimetre waves technology in this three-minute podcast. Award-winning Air Cargo Scanner (video)This two-minute video discusses the work of Dr Brian Sowerby, Chief Research Scientist at CSIRO Minerals, and the team that developed the Air Cargo Scanner for the Detection of Contraband in Air Cargo which won the team a 2006 CSIRO Medal for Research Achievement. CSIRO demonstrates world’s fastest wireless link (Media release 6 Dec 06)CSIRO researchers will tomorrow demonstrate the fastest and most efficient wireless link ever achieved. Dr Paul Jackway: making microscopes work harderDr Paul Jackway’s research in image analysis and automated microscopy is finding new ways to rapidly analyse cells and other biological samples with minimal human intervention.
