TechnologiesMining &amp; MineralsBuilding &amp; ConstructionFire Safety &amp; ControlTransportUrban PlanningUtilitiesTransport &amp; Infrastructure Subscription Information Media Release&#160;&#160;&#160;Dr Nigel Johnson (L) with the Vice President of The Textile Institute (Southern Australia), Ashley van Krieken.‘Service to Industry Award’ to textile division ChiefReference: 06/255CSIRO Textile and Fibre Technology’s Chief, Dr Nigel Johnson, has been presented with a ‘Service to Industry Award’ for his long-term commitment to the textile industry.7 December 2006Nominated by industry members, the Award was announced earlier this month at the Annual General Meeting of The Textile Institute&#8217;s Southern Australia Section. &#8220;Your long-term service to the industry, both in Australia and overseas, as a long standing member of The Textile Institute and the industry networks that you have developed are recognised in addition to the industry initiatives instigated during your time as Chief at CSIRO Textile and Fibre Technology,&#8221; said Textile Institute (Southern Aust) President, Les Mapstone. The Institute&#8217;s Vice President, Ashley van Krieken, stressed the Service to Industry Award was not necessarily made yearly but, rather, was presented when the merits of a particular nominee warranted it. The industry commended Dr Johnson for his overall contribution in providing direction and innovation in various senior positions within major national and international organisations throughout his career including: The International Wool Secretariat in Ilkley, UK; The School of Textile Technology at the University of NSW; The Wool Research Organisation of New Zealand Inc (WRONZ), which became Canesis Network Ltd (including being Managing Director of Canesis Australia Pty Ltd); and CSIRO Textile and Fibre Technology. Mr van Krieken commended Dr Johnson for his focus on partnerships with industry and his emphasis on commercial outcomes. &#8220;The industry commended Dr Johnson for his overall contribution in providing direction and innovation in various senior positions within major national and international organisations throughout his career &#8221; The Textile Institute is a chartered professional organization serving the global textiles, clothing and footwear industries, with members in over 90 countries. Its mission is to promote professionalism in all areas associated with these industries. Dr Johnson said he was honoured to receive the Award. "Although it has faced major challenges over the past 30 years, there is now a thriving textile and fibre industry in Australasia," he said. &#160; "I have great admiration for those in business with the vision and courage to adopt new business approaches, launch new products and develop new markets. It has been most rewarding to be involved with organisations that assist these business innovators to succeed."
