Are you currently undertaking&#160;tertiary studies, in science, engineering or other science related areas? If you are, than Australia's largest employer of scientist, CSIRO, could have&#160;an opportunity that suits your professional development at one&#160;one of our research Divisions. CSIRO has different programs for high school students, graduates and undergraduates. Highschool students CSIRO offer students from Australian high schools in years 10, 11 or 12, the opportunity for a work experience placement within a research Division. You will gain a practical understanding of the different types of project work in a research area which may help you when making career decisions. How to apply: High schools students can organise a work experience placement through their school co-ordinator as part of a recognised work-experience program.&#160; They will assist you to negotiate a placement with the research Division that fits with your interests.&#160;Each research Division has a Human Resource Manager who will be able to help you and your school co-ordinator arrange a suitable placement. Industrial trainees/undergraduates CSIRO offers industrial traineeships to selected tertiary students studying science, engineering or other relevant fields. This provides an opportunity to combine work experience with formal training under the supervision of a scientist within a research Division. The work experience can be for varying periods up to a maximum of 12 months. Trainees are not employed by CSIRO, but in some cases you may receive a stipend and/or a contribution towards living costs. How to apply: You should discuss your interest in an industrial traineeship with your university advisor and/or a research scientist or Human Resource Manager in the relevant research Division. Volunteer fellowships/graduates If you are a science or engineering graduate who has little or no work experience, or who has experience but is temporarily unemployed, then this scheme may assist you to enhance your skills and keep abreast of developments in your field.&#160; CSIRO offers the&#160;opportunity to work for 6-12 months as volunteer professional under the supervision of a research scientist.&#160;A training program is devised in advance between you and the relevant research Division. This is designed to enhance your competitiveness in applying for paid employment. Volunteer fellows are unpaid and are not employees of CSIRO. How to apply: You should discuss your interest in a volunteer fellowship with a CSIRO research scientist or the research Division that most closely aligns with your area of research.
