Current activities Background Academic qualifications&#160; Achievements&#160; Current activities Dr&#160;Daniel Walker is the Program Leader for Resource Futures, CSIRO Sustainable Ecosystems, and he&#160;plays a lead role in CSIRO&#8217;s emerging strategy&#160;to encourage&#160;more effective engagement with Indigenous communities.&#160; He is also a member of the editorial board of the journal Agricultural Systems. Background Dr Walker has a background in international research in natural resource management and human ecology.&#160; He joined CSIRO in Townsville, Queensland,&#160;as a research scientist in 1994 and has been Resource Futures Program Leader since 2003.&#160; He was the Regional Planning &amp; Management Theme Leader for the Co-operative Research Centre (CRC) for Tropical Savannas from 2000 to 2003. Dr Walker's key areas of expertise include: research management systems analysis of regional dynamics development &amp; implementation of decision support systems for adaptive natural resource management&#160;and capacity building participatory research methodologies indigenous &amp; scientific knowledge integration. The Resource Futures Program improves the long-term prosperity and quality of life of Australians by helping communities to use resources to greater sustainable net benefit. Academic qualifications Dr Walker completed a Bachelor of Science at the University of Edinburgh, Scotland, UK in 1988. The following year he completed a first class honours degree in agriculture, forestry and rural economy from the same institution.&#160; He was awarded a PhD from the&#160;University of Wales, UK, in 1994 with his thesis A&#160;knowledge based system approach to agroforestry research and extension. Dr Walker&#160;also graduated from the Australian&#160;Institute of Company Directors. Achievements Dr Walker established&#160;the Herbert Resource Information Centre (HRIC), a ground-breaking ten-year collaborative venture between industry, local government, and community&#160;which&#160;is enabling a rural community to use advanced technologies and R&amp;D outputs in regional development. National dissemination of the generic model developed from the HRIC experience is resulting in investment by 22 regional groups around Australia, and significant policy influence in South Australia, Western Australia and Queensland. He has also conducted analyses and facilitated negotiations in the sugar industry that have: provided a potential future for the A$20&#160;million per annum&#160;sugar industry on the Queensland Sunshine Coast developed methods for supporting other rural industries under severe financial, community, and legislative pressure. Dr Walker has received the following awards: Stewart McDougall Prize &amp; the Stebbing Medal for Silviculture James Anthony More Memorial Prize for Agriculture University of Edinburgh Bursary.
